Nir operates on a **Hybrid Model** connecting three core pillars:

1.  [cite_start]**Diversified Water Sources:** Rainwater Harvesting, Fog Nets, Atmospheric Water Generators (AWG), and Greywater Recycling[cite: 43].

## ğŸ› ï¸ Hardware Modules

### Module 1: Household Water Security
Designed to provide year-round water and reduce the burden on women.
* [cite_start]**Rainwater Harvesting (RWH):** Rooftop catchment with low-cost filtration (biosand)[cite: 101, 102].
* [cite_start]**Fog Harvesting:** Polypropylene mesh nets installed in highlands to trap fog droplets (150-750L/day potential) [cite: 127-129].
* [cite_start]**Atmospheric Water Generator (AWG):** Solar-powered community units condensing moisture from humid air ($25-32^{\circ}C$, 70-75% RH)[cite: 154].
* [cite_start]**Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR):** A dual-system collecting RWH overflow and filtered greywater to recharge the groundwater table [cite: 167-169].

### Module 2: Agricultural Water Security
[cite_start]Targeting the 90.6% reliance on groundwater for irrigation in the Barind region[cite: 177].
* [cite_start]**Khari Revival:** Re-excavating traditional canals and ponds for surface water storage[cite: 183].
* [cite_start]**River Water Supply:** A logistics network connecting farmers to nearby river sources via water entrepreneurs[cite: 193].
